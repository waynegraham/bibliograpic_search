Emily Lorraine de Montluzin

Volume 210 (1861)

  • 210 (1861) 2 S "St. Hugh of Lincoln."

    Alfred John Dunkin.

    Orig. "Delfra Niknud", which is an anagram of "Alfred Dunkin"
  • 210 (1861): 74-75. A: "Hachettes Diluviennes et Industrie Primitive."

    Jean Beno"t Dsir Cochet, Abb Cochet.

    [Orig. "L'Abbe Cochet"]
  • 210 (1861): 75-76. L: "'W.S.N.' and the 'National' Reviewer."

    Rev. James Craigie Robertson.

    [Orig. "J. C. Robertson"; refers to the controversy re the proper citation of the name of Thomas à Becket; dated Canterbury, where Robertson, the author of a biography of Thomas à Becket, was a canon]
  • 210 (1861): 76-77. L: "[Description of] St. Frideswide's Shrine, Oxford Cathedral."

    Thomas Orlando Sheldon Jewitt.

    [Orig. "O. Jewitt"; Jewitt was an illustrator of antiquarian books (BMGC).]
  • 210 (1861): 77. L: "Ancient Lake-dwellers."

    Sir William Robert Wills Wilde.

    [Orig. "W. R. Wilde"]
  • 210 (1861): 180-181. L: "The Architect of Lincoln Cathedral."

    Charles Forbes Ren de Tryon, Comte de Montalembert.

    [Orig. "Le Comte de Montalembert"]
  • 210 (1861): 190. L: "The Liverpool Free Public Library."

    Sir James Allanson Picton.

    [Orig. "J. A. Picton, Chairman of the Committee"; dated Liverpool, Picton's home]
  • 210 (1861): 253-259. A: "On Archeo-geology": "Antediluvian Hatchets and Primitive Industry. A Report addressed to the Prefect of the Seine-Infrieure, by the Abb Cochet, Inspector of Historical Monuments."

    Jean Beno"t Dsir Cochet, Abb Cochet.

    [Orig. "The Abb Cochet"]
  • 210 (1861): 312-313. L: "The Architecture of Lincoln Cathedral."

    John Henry Parker.

    [Orig. "J. H. Parker"; dated Oxford, where Parker was a bookseller]
  • 210 (1861): 439. L: "Quarry in Window of Chapel, East Hendred House, Berks."

    Charles Alban Buckler.

    [Orig. "C. A. Buckler"; dated Oxford, where Buckler was a member of the Architectural Society]
  • 210 (1861): 594. L: "The Earldom of Derby."

    Llewellynn Frederick William Jewitt.

    [Orig. "Ll. Jewitt"; dated Derby, where Jewitt edited the Derby Telegraph]
  • 210 (1861) 677-678 L "Preservation of Stone."

    Rev. Harry Longueville Jones.

    Orig. "H.L.J."
  • 211 (1861) 139-141 L "Memorial from the Bishop of St. David's [Anthony Rudd] and others to Lord Burghleigh [in 1595]."

    Sir Henry Ellis.

    Orig. "H.E."; re an Elizabethan MS in the British Museum; Ellis was principal librarian of the British Museum.
  • 211 (1861): 160-162. A: "Berwickshire Naturalists' Club [re 'A Field-day at Linhope']."

    Frederick Richard Wilson.

    [Orig. "F. R. Wilson, Architect, Alnwick"]
  • 211 (1861): 254-256. A: "Note on Bronze Marmites, often met with in Archaeological Collections; with special reference to one found at Caudebec-les-Elbeuf, in 1861."

    Jean Beno"t Dsir Cochet, Abb Cochet.

    [Orig. "L'Abbe Cochet"]
  • 211 (1861): 422. L: "Birth-place of Wycliffe."

    Rev. William Barton Caparn.

    [Orig. "W. B. Caparn"; dated Draycot, Wells, Somersetshire, where Caparn was vicar]
  • 211 (1861): 423. L: "Marmites."

    John Obadiah Westwood.

    [Orig. "J. O. Westwood"]
  • 211 (1861): 424. L: "'Biforietta and Witta.'"

    John Obadiah Westwood.

    [Orig. "J. O. Westwood"]

    [Orig. "E. J. Thackwell, Barrister-at-law"; Thackwell signed his full name to an item in GM 213 (1862): 344.]
  • 211 (1861): 489-494. A: "Note on a Christian Grave of the Middle Ages, Found at Etaples, in 1861."

    Jean Beno"t Dsir Cochet, Abb Cochet.
